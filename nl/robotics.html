Upcoming Workshops in Robotics

Let's make Christmas trees sing!

You will learn, step by step with us, how to make your plant (mini Xmas tree) twinkle, sing (no kidding) and/or alert you when it needs watering, by using an Arduino board to measure the humidity of the ground and to produce various sounds accordingly.

When? Saturday, December 12, 2015, from 9am to 5pm

Where? Co.Station, Place Sainte-Gudule 5, 1000 Brussels

Who can attend? From 16 to 99 y.o. Kids coming with their parent(s) are also welcome. No previous experience in electronics nor IT is required, just an open, curious & creative mind ! :)

How much? The workshop is free. You will be able to buy the material (including the Arduino board) at the end of the day at a discounted price of 50 euros.

Build Your Own Robotic Arm

For our first robotic workshop, we'll build a robotic arm! Sure, our arm won't be as sophisticated as the ones you see in movies, but we promise it'll be mighty impressive! To build it, we'll use lots of cool stuff, including an Arduino (a small computer which holds in your hand), servomotors, popsicle sticks, soldering irons... And to guide us, we'll have the great maker-artist-inventor master... Julien Dutertre.

When? Wednesday, November 4, 2015, from 9am to 5pm

Where? Co.Station, Place Sainte-Gudule 5, 1000 Brussels

Who can attend? Teenagers (boys and girls), from 13 to 17 years old
